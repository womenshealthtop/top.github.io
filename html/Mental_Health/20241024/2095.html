Mental Health and Perinatal Families
Source: womenshealth.top Author: admin Published date: 2024-10-24

 The journey into parenthood, whether through pregnancy, childbirth, or adoption, is a profound experience filled with joy, anticipation, and often overwhelming challenges. During the Perinatal period (includes conception through the first year of a child's life) physical health is often the primary focus during this time, however, mental health is equally critical for both parents and their new child. Understanding the importance of mental well-being can pave the way for a healthier, happier family.

Understanding Perinatal Mental Health dedecms.com

Perinatal mental health refers to the emotional well-being of parents during pregnancy and the postpartum period. This phase can be marked by significant hormonal changes, sleep deprivation, and the stress of adjusting to new responsibilities. Conditions such as postpartum depression, anxiety, and even psychosis can arise, affecting not just the parent but the entire family dynamic.
